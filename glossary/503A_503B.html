What Are 503A and 503B?

Sections 503A and 503B of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic (FD&C) Act define two categories of compounding pharmacies under U.S. law.

  • Who they are: Local or community pharmacies that prepare custom medications for individual patients based on a prescription.
  • Purpose: To provide personalized drug formulations when commercial products don‚Äôt meet patient needs.
  • Key rules: Must be patient-specific; regulated mainly by state boards; not required to follow full FDA cGMP standards; cannot make drugs in bulk or for resale.

üíä Section 503B ‚Äî Outsourcing Facilities

  • Who they are: Larger facilities that can compound sterile drugs in bulk without individual prescriptions.
  • Purpose: To supply hospitals, clinics, or offices with ready-to-use sterile preparations.
  • Key rules: Must register with the FDA, follow full cGMP, and are inspected regularly. Products are not FDA-approved for safety or efficacy.
In short: 503A = individualized, prescription-based compounding. 503B = large-scale, quality-controlled compounding under FDA oversight.
