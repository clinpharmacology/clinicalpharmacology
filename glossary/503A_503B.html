Risks and Adverse Events

Even under GMP, 503B drugs can cause adverse reactions, especially in sterile routes (IV, intrathecal, ophthalmic). Potential risks include:

  • Pyrogenic/endotoxin reactions (fever, shock)
  • Particulate contamination ‚Üí embolism, phlebitis
  • Concentration errors ‚Üí overdosing or underdosing
  • Excipient incompatibility ‚Üí precipitation, instability
  • Allergic reactions
  • Microbial contamination ‚Üí sepsis, meningitis
FDA Reporting & Historical Context

Adverse events must be reported via FDA MedWatch. Historical incidents like the 2012 NECC fungal meningitis outbreak led to creation of Section 503B to enforce GMP.
